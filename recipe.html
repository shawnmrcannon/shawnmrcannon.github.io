Chicken Marsala Recipe

  • 1-1 ½ lbs of boneless chicken breast
  • 3 tbl spoons flour
  • Salt: 2tbls spoons recommend
  • Pepper:2 tbls spoons recomended
  • 3 tbls spoons of butter
  • 1 table spoon olive oil
  • 8 oz pre sliced mushrooms (bella or button)
  • 1 finely cut shallot
  • 2 heaps minced garlic
  • 2/3 cup Chicken Broth
  • 2/3 Cup Marsala Wine
  • 2/3 cup heavy cream (recommend to purchase a half pint)
  • 2 teaspoons thyme (recommend fresh)
  • 2 table spoons parsley (Garnish/optional)
  • Optional : Burbon or a dry Red wine

  1. Optional Slice chicken breasts

  2. Tenderize (roughly ¼ inch thick)

  3. Place flour, ¾ salt (to taste), ¾ Pepper (to taste) in Ziplock (recommend Gallon/freezer). Add all chicken to bag and shake thoroughly

  4. Heat olive oil and 2 tbl spoons of butter in Larger NON STICK skillet over med-high

  5. Place flour dusted chicken in pan (shake extra flour off)

  6. Cook for 6 minutes turning once

  7. Transfer to plate and set aside. In the same pan add remaining butter and add mushrooms

  8. Add Shallots, garlic, and ¼ teaspoon of saltCook 1-2 minutes

  9. Add broth, wine, heavy cream, thyme, and salt and pepper to taste. Recommend using a wood spoon for scrapping any browned funguys from bottom of pan. Bring liquid to a boil, reduce heat to medium

  10. Continue to gently boil until sauce is reduced to ½

  11. Add chicken back into sauce and reduce heat to low. Simmer until sauce thickens (2-3 minutes)

  12. Sprinkle with Parsley and serve!

This Chicken Marsala recipe is straight forward and simple. You can follow the steps above and create a wonderful dish! However, I added some extra optional steps and advise to make your dinner feel like it is served at a five-star restaurant without breaking the bank.
If you want your sauce to be a bit thicker, you can add 2 teaspoons of corn starch during the thickening process, at the risk of loosing a little flavor. It is important to whisk corn starch thoroughly to create a thick sauce without clumps.
You can also add a liquor to your sauce to give it an extra kick. It is recommended to use a dry red wine or whiskey.
This dish is best served this pasta, rice, or mashed potatoes. I find that the mashed potatoes offer a great complement to the overall texture and taste of the sauce. There is also the added benefit of having already opened a heavy whipping cream. The remaining cream from a half pint container is just enough to add to your potatoes to make them nice and fluffy.
The easiest step to miss in this process is adding thyme. If you forget that your marsala will be lacking in flavor. Much like in coding; sometimes missing a seemingly insignificant step can have disappointing results.

Midnight Mule

  • 1 shot of Black berry Moonshine
  • ½ shot Lavender syrup
  • ¼ lemon
  • Ginger beer

  1. Chill mug (best served in copper mug but can be served out of any drinking cup)

  2. Pour moonshine and lavender syrup into a shaker, ¾ filled with ice.

  3. Squeeze lemon juice (do not worry about the pips)

  4. Shake

  5. Pour into mug with grated top still on shaker to avoid ice and pips

  6. Pour ginger beer

  7. Pour ginger beer and Enjoy!
